Miscellaneous

Restoring and colorizing vintage Indian photos is an endeavor I have loved the most, but it requires significant effort as rarely anyone else is taking on this important task. I have tried many approaches to obtain the original high-resolution photos needed to do quality work. This has involved contacting libraries and museums to find prints in their collections. However, most photos available online are too low-resolution to work with effectively. Sometimes I was successful in acquiring an original, other times I had to upscale lower-quality images using software like Topaz to make them suitable for editing. The editing process in Photoshop is extensive. It involves carefully removing scratches, dust, tears and other damage, which sometimes requires reconstructing parts that were too far gone. Lighting, shadows and contrast must be corrected to improve the visual quality. Colorization is especially labor-intensive, requiring research to determine accurate historic colors. Layers upon layers are built up as each element is meticulously colored to be as true to the original as possible, despite the inherent limitations. While the colors can never be perfectly reproduced, striving for accuracy honors the history captured in these photos. Here all are downscaled to less than 1000-pixel height and width.
