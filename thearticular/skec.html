For my sketches I use various tools to bring my drawings to life. When it comes to pencils, I have a range of options. The HB pencil is great for general sketching, while the 2B pencil provides darker lines and shading. For lighter lines, I use the 2H pencil. When I want to add depth and texture, I turn to charcoal pencils, such as the soft charcoal for rich blacks and the hard charcoal for finer details. To correct mistakes, I rely on kneaded erasers that can be shaped and molded. For smudging and blending, I use tortillons or blending stumps. Finaly, I scan them at highest resolution, and correct any distortion or bad colors using photoshop. All the images here are downscaled to less than 800-pixel height and width, to not slow down the site. Finer details might not be visible.
